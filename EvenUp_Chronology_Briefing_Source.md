### The big idea
A medical chronology turns thousands of pages of records into the backbone of a case — the timeline that drives evaluation, damages, negotiation, and trial prep. EvenUp's MedChrons build that fast. The whole game is whether the firm *trusts* the output enough to rely on it. Trust is earned through verification, not assumed.

### Why this workflow first
Medical chronologies are high-frequency, high-friction, and high-value. They're the perfect bounded pilot: prove the workflow with one cohort, measure it, and expand on evidence. The MedChron is the first repeatable adoption play, not the last.

### Ready → Review → Act (the workflow the module teaches)
- **Ready:** a MedChron is only as good as its packet — records, bills, provider list, treatment window, correct matter, named files. Garbage in, garbage out.
- **Review:** orient on the timeline; verify the high-stakes facts against citations; filter and cross-check visit counts; hunt for gaps and pre-existing conditions; spot transposed dates and merged encounters.
- **Act:** turn findings into action — records requests, treatment-gap follow-up, escalate causation or pre-existing issues to the attorney, prep attorney review, and document in the firm's system.

### The failure modes worth naming
Transposed or wrong dates; two encounters merged into one; a missing provider or record; mischaracterized severity; a citation that doesn't support the claim. Every one is catchable in seconds if you know to look — and that's what turns a skeptic into a daily user.

### What it does for the firm and the client
Done right, the paralegal moves from manual extraction to verification and case-story development; the attorney walks into negotiation prepared; missing records and gaps surface early instead of in demand prep. The result is faster, stronger cases — and more time for the human work clients actually need.
