## PASTE THIS INTO THE "CUSTOMIZE" FIELD

Create a clean, professional **explainer slide deck** titled **"Responsible GenAI for Lawyers: Prompt, Verify, Protect."**

**Audience:** attorneys and legal staff at a personal-injury / plaintiff firm who are starting to use generative AI. **Tone:** practical, confident, plain-language — a trusted colleague, not a hype reel. **Visual style:** minimal and corporate (clean type, simple icons, lots of white space). One clear idea per slide. No jargon-as-decoration, no cute illustrations.

**Follow this slide order:**
1. **Title** — Responsible GenAI for Lawyers: Prompt, Verify, Protect.
2. **The stakes** — lawyers have been sanctioned for filing AI-fabricated citations; the failure was the missing *process*, not the tool.
3. **The three habits** — Prompt · Verify · Protect (overview).
4. **Prompt: the CLAIM framework** — Context, Legal task, Audience, Instructions, Mode of output.
5. **Prompt: vague vs. structured** — show a weak prompt ("summarize these records") beside a CLAIM prompt that produces a reviewable medical-chronology table.
6. **Verify: ground it in the record** — point the AI at the actual documents; ask it to cite sources and flag uncertainty.
7. **Verify: spot-check and own it** — check the high-stakes facts; a human lawyer reviews and owns the final work product.
8. **Protect: confidentiality** — mask PHI and client identifiers, share only what's needed, use a purpose-built legal platform over a consumer chatbot.
9. **The one rule** — "The AI drafts. The lawyer decides."
10. **Recap** — Prompt well, verify always, protect the client.

Keep each slide to a short headline plus one or two supporting lines. Pull only from the provided source; do not invent statistics, case names, or citations.
